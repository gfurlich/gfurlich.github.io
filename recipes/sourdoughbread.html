Greg's Sourdough Bread

Makes 1 Loaf

Greg's Tried and Tested Sourdough Bread

This recipe was inspired and modified from The Perfect Loaf. Multiple day process: start with feeding starter, next day prepare dough, last day bake. Good weekend activity.
Dough Prep: 2 hrs
Ferment: Overnight
12 - 24 hrs
Baking: 55 min
30 covered
25 uncovered
Total: Multiple Days
Difficulty: Advanced

Ingredients (by weight)

  • 500 g All Purpose Flour
  • 135 g Heavy Flour
    (Rye, Whole Wheat, Buck Wheat, Pumperknickel)
  • 30 g Vital Wheat Gluten
  • 13 g (~2 Tbsp) Kosher Salt
  • 80 g Sourdough Starter
  • 425 g Purified Water
    heated to ~90 - 105 °F

Instructions

  1. Combine flours and salt. Mix
  2. Mix in water to form raggy dough. More water may be needed for different flours.
  3. Stretch and fold 6 times with 15, 15, 15, 30, 30, 30 minutes between each stretvh and fold.
  4. Pre-form on Counter (Pre-shape)
  5. Form into boule or batard using envelope method. Add toppings if desired. Place in banneton, overnight in fridge.
  6. Preheat Oven to 450° with Dutch Oven for ~1 hr.
  7. Place in Dutch Oven, cover for 30 min. Uncover and bake for 25 more.
Tips:
  1. Feed starter the day before, do not discard a portion to allow it to grow quickly overnight.
  2. Top with black and white sesame seeds. Wet hand with water and rub over top to help toppings adhere<./li>
  3. Works well with squid ink to color the bread black. Do not add salt then as squid ink is saline.

Feeding Sourdough Starter

Ingredients (by weight)

  • 50 g All Purpose Flour
  • 5 g Rye Flour
  • 5 g Vital Wheat Gluten
  • 40 g Purified Water
    heated to ~90 - 105 °F

Instructions

  1. Discard half of the sourdough starter. (There are many options of what to do with the discard, I make a pancake with mine and feed to the chickens.)
  2. Add in dry ingredients and mix
  3. Add in water and mix. Should be at the stage that still retains shape but not too dry.
  4. Let sit out of direct sun until next feeding, 2-3 days.
